1. Social media is a tool - use it wisely.

Social media is far from being all bad.

There are often tangible benefits that follow from responsible and conscious social media use. Many people log on to social media for a sense of belonging, self-expression, curiosity, or a desire to connect. The main advantage of social media is digital connectivity with geographically dispersed acquaintances and friends, the ability to find interesting events or places, communicate with like-minded others around our interests.

Social media can also be used for noble causes, create awareness, build communities around the way people live, build a personal brand, and much more.

It is also hard to remember dates like birthdays and is a good idea to wish people I wish to stay connected with on their birthdays via Messenger/WhatsApp.

Nowadays, I mostly use Twitter (using curated lists instead of following other accounts), Instagram (where I am extremely selective about following and giving access to my profile). Once in a while, I log in to Facebook, Snapchat, however, these days they provide little to no value. Facebook's algorithm is so bad that the news feed rarely provides any value.

When using social media following the right accounts and curating the feeds on Instagram/Twitter is very important as ultimately our thoughts and actions are shaped by what we consume.

If someone needs to reach out to me or after several years, Facebook, Instagram messaging may provide great value for that purpose. Responses might be delayed though as I only log in once or twice a day for a limited time, that too on my computer and not phone (unless I am traveling).

2. Avoid Manipulation & Embrace Reality - Nullius in verba

Algorithmically curated feeds = Echo-chamber of Confirmation Bias.

Social media increases the returns to intolerance. Fake news, algorithmically curated feeds are undermining the ‚Äútruth‚Äù. People choose various types of content to post on different social media platforms and their posts may not always present an accurate picture about their life or this world. In the court of social media, context is inadmissible. Social media also tends to make every problem, your problem by showing snippets about an issue.

Furthermore, a lot of people (not everyone) do tend to glamorize their lives sometimes to seek attention or avoid facing their insecurities and weaknesses in the real world. We humans are like moon, we only show our shining side on social media.

According to Maslow's hierarchy of needs, people post due to their needs of connecting with other humans, to feel some kind of social acceptance from a group or individual by sharing their accomplishments and opinions, for self-actualization to feel rewarded by bragging or showing off, promote an agenda or political rant to feel relevant.

Instagram makes people seem better-looking and happier than they really are which can have negative effects on the psyche of followers who might buy into it. It's also important to recognize that two people on the internet could perceive the same post differently, a lot of times out of context.

Therefore, I must consciously post on social media and never make an opinion about someone based on their social media posts or engagement.

I also hope that others do not think my life is better or worse than their life based on what I occasionally share on the internet. Ultimately a good & fulfilling life is meant to be filled with constant short term struggles that build one's character in the long term; it's just that we all go through different struggles in different phases of our life and social media does not provide an accurate representation of the same.

3. Be selectively social - accept requests & follow cautiously.

Social Media Friends ‚â† Friends.

Most people often label others that they shared some good amount of time with during a certain phase of life as "friends", consume their activity on social media, which if not done thoughtfully can lead to continuous behavior modification without them realizing its short & long-term consequences.

Engaging with people just by consuming their stories, liking their posts, projecting your life is NOT staying in touch. Authentically staying in touch requires deliberate effort from both individuals to share their experiences via calls and messages, organizing meetups, or virtual hangouts. If two people just "connect" merely via social media platforms, are they connected? Probably not.

Therefore, it is very important to assess if just a short-term or long term acquaintance and give them access to my profile and follow them accordingly.

"People come into your life for a Reason, a Season, or a Lifetime...." - <Author Unknown>

4. Curate Lists

Everyone evolves with time.

Different people play different roles in life, some assist through a difficulty, some push you to a greater level, some teach lifetime lessons, some give an unbelievable amount of joy. My job is to love the person, cherish those moments, apply the lesson in life.

That being said, people are also often shaped by their experiences which are determined by a lot of factors such as their upbringing, opinions, circle of influence, media consumption, etc. all of which are highly variable with time. So, a person that I might have resonated with or admired at some point might have a different personality and traits a few years later and if not in contact, we might not know each other anymore. People that I don't know must not have access to my posts which provide insights into my life and thoughts.

Therefore, it's a good idea to re-evaluate and curate the list of friends/followers periodically and disconnect digitally based on factors like how much mutual interest is present which can be determined by personal interactions via in-person meetings, messages, and calls over last few years.

If reconnecting is needed, we can reconnect, but generally fewer people online and in life result in less distractions and drama further leading to a sound mind and positive-sum games with long-term people.

5. Be Mostly Private - Restrain posts & stories.

Private Digital Life ‚áí Peaceful Life.

I must only choose to post on social media when I have something to communicate, share an experience and never - to show off, get attention, justify or seek validation. I must be open & vulnerable with (most) people in-person and the opposite on the internet or social media.

Occasionally, I may share information that adds value to others' life or support a good cause without oversharing about it.

Anything beyond that is not needed because what happens in my day-to-day life, what I do during the day when I travel etc. should not be of concern to anyone outside my immediate family and select friends & acquaintances in my current phase of life with whom I shall remain in contact primarily via calls and texts.

6. Think, Reevaluate and Archive

Share the ongoing & seal the finished.

Before posting, I must think about why I am posting what I want to post and try to delay the post to make sure its not impulsive. I think there might come a time in future where I won't be posting anything at all about myself on social media.

After a few days (and in select cases, few months) posts shared on social media eventually become irrelevant. Most (not all) people do not truly care about other's past or present; they might be curious but that's different from truly caring. Even if they do care, it is hard to know about a person merely being a social media connection.

If someone truly cares, they will eventually contact me directly and I shall do the same for those I care about irrespective of social media presence.

Furthermore, the algorithms only tend to show posts for about 3 to 5 days in the feed after which a post or highlight remains useful only to people who are digital stalkers. So, it is probably a good idea to archive posts within 5 days.

7. No social media apps on phone

Nothing to really miss out on.

Constant checking of social media apps on phones is driven by dopamine release, fear of missing out, conscious and unconscious comparison with people based on the posts that might not tell truth about life. According to various studies, this has lead to wasted time, lost attention span, biased views, feeling disconnected and, bad mental health among many individuals.

Social media is engineered to make it as addictive as possible to maximize the profit that can be extracted via user's attention and data.

To prevent the negative effects, I must not have any social media apps on my phone (unless I am traveling and do not have access to my laptop) and have a daily allowance of about 15-20 mins for relevant social media consumption. When I do have to post stories, I may use my apps on my computer to do so. This will also make sure that I do not indulge in impulsive posting on social media.

8. Hibernate & Refresh

Once every year, I must isolate myself from all social media by temporarily deactivating accounts, deleting past activity and messages, re-evaluating lists based on online/offline interaction and mutual interest to clean up social media feed and rejuvenate.
